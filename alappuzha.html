Alappuzha

Alappuzha, also known as Alleppey, is a coastal city in Kerala, India. It is famous for its backwaters, lagoons, and houseboats. The city has a beautiful beach, Alappuzha Beach, which is a popular tourist spot. The annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race is a major event that takes place in the city. Alappuzha is a culturally rich destination that offers a unique blend of natural beauty and traditional Kerala hospitality.

Arthunkal Church

The Arthunkal Church, also known as the St. Andrew's Forane Church, is a famous Christian pilgrimage site. It is known for its beautiful architecture, religious significance, and the annual Arthunkal Perunnal festival celebrated in January.

Krishnapuram Palace

The Krishnapuram Palace is a historic palace known for its exquisite architecture, beautiful murals, and the museum showcasing the artifacts and heritage of the region. It is a popular tourist destination and a great spot for history and culture enthusiasts.

Alappuzha Beach

The Alappuzha Beach is a picturesque beach located in Alappuzha, Kerala, India, known for its serene atmosphere, golden sand, and palm-lined shores. It is great spot for leisure activities like swimming, sunbathing, and boating.

Kuttanad Backwaters

The Kuttanad Backwaters are a network of interconnected lagoons, lakes, and canals located in the Kuttanad region of Kerala, India, known for its scenic beauty, traditional houseboats, and serene atmosphere.

Vembanad Lake

Vembanad Lake is the largest lake in Kerala, India, spanning several districts and known for its scenic beauty, tranquil atmosphere, and rich biodiversity. It is a popular tourist destination and a hub for several water-based activities.
