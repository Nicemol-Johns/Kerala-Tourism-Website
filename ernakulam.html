Districts > Ernakulam

Ernakulam

Ernakulam is a district located in the central part of the Indian state of Kerala. It is the commercial and financial capital of the state. The city is home to several famous landmarks and is also famous for its seafood cuisine, which is popular among locals and tourists alike. Overall, Ernakulam is a bustling city that offers a perfect blend of modernity and tradition.

Fort Kochi

Fort Kochi is a seaside town in Kochi, Kerala, India, known for its rich cultural heritage, historic landmarks, and beautiful beaches. It is a popular tourist destination and a hub for art, music, and cultural events.

Hill Palace Museum

The Hill Palace Museum showcases the artifacts and heritage of the former Maharaja of Kochi. It is known for its extensive collection of antiquities, paintings, and artifacts that offer a glimpse into the rich cultural history of Kerala.

Paradesi Synagogue

The Paradesi Synagogue is a historic Jewish synagogue located in Kochi, India, known for its unique architecture and religious significance.

Dutch Palace

The Dutch Palace, also known as Mattancherry Palace, is a historical palace located in Kochi, Kerala, India. Built by the Portuguese and renovated by the Dutch, it is now a museum that showcases the rich cultural heritage of the region.

Bolghatti Palace

Bolghatty Palace is a historical palace located on the Bolghatty Island in Kochi, Kerala, India. Built in 1744 by the Dutch, it has been converted into a heritage hotel and is a popular tourist attraction.
